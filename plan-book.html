Project Summary

The Cascade 480 is a 480-square-foot accessory dwelling unit designed for cost-efficient construction and maximum builder margin. Every element has been engineered around standard material dimensions to minimize waste, reduce labor, and accelerate build timelines. The plan set is suitable for owner-builder projects, spec home builders, and production ADU contractors.

Design Philosophy: No special-order materials. No engineered lumber. No steel. No custom windows. Every dimension is a multiple of 4 feet. Every wall section falls on 16-inch centers. A framing crew with standard tools and experience can build this.

Construction Schedule & Build Phases
Sheet C-001

8-Week Build Timeline

Based on a 3-person crew working standard hours. Assumes slab cure time, no weather delays, and materials on site Day 1.

Wk 1
Foundation
Excavation, formwork, rebar, pour, cure. Underground plumbing stubs placed. Anchor bolts set.
Wk 2
Framing
Walls framed, sheathed, stood. Trusses delivered and set. Roof sheathing. Housewrap.
Wk 3
Roof + Windows
Roofing installed. Windows and exterior doors set. Siding begins. Exterior waterproofed.
Wk 4–5
Rough-Ins
Plumbing rough-in. Electrical rough-in. HVAC mini-split lines. Insulation. Inspections.
Wk 6–7
Drywall + Finish
Hang, tape, texture. Paint. LVP flooring. Cabinets. Trim. Interior doors. Fixtures.
Wk 8
Trim + Punch
Final electrical trim. Plumbing trim. Punch list. Final clean. Walkthrough.

Critical Path Notes

Week 1: Order trusses Day 1 — lead time is typically 1–2 weeks.
Week 2: Schedule rough-in inspections before starting insulation (Week 5).
Week 4: Order cabinets and countertops — lead time for prefab is 2–3 weeks.
Week 5: Schedule insulation and drywall inspections. Coordinate drywall delivery.
Week 7: Final inspection scheduling — book 1 week in advance.
